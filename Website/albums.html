Band: Modest Mouse ‎
Album: The Moon & Antarctica
Label: Up
Released: November 18, 1997
Genre: Indie rock, Alternative Rock
Length: 73:58

     Its hard for me to pick only one Modest Mouse album for this list, but if I had to choose from one of their first fantastic four albums then this one would win by a small margin. Now on their first major label debut with Epic, Modest Mouse set on to expand their already muscular sound with more experimentation and greater-than-life themes. Most of the songs are contemplative in nature, detailing our existence in this planet, all told with Isaac Brock's (main singer) unique, off putting to some, vocals and best lyrics to date. This is one of the first albums that showed me the effect that music could have on people, that there is a world of music out there than what you typically hear on the radio or the usual bands that every kid who liked rock must have liked in the early 2000's. It opened my ears to pay more attention to the lyrics of every song that had them, to the instrumentation, and to every little detail of a music album that made for a hollistic experience.
